Frameworks in the Wild

The University of Waikato's Graduate Attributes were published in 2016 as part of a wider curriculum review. While they do not explicitly mention digital literacy, on reading it will become obvious that many of the attributes are closely related. In this activity, you will look specifically at the second dimension of the HeDiCom framework, Empowering students for a digital society, and compare those competencies with those of the Graduate Attributes.

To Do:

  1. Remind yourself of the five Graduate Attributes.
  2. Familiarise yourself with the behavioural indicators for the Empowering students for a digital society dimension.
  3. Add a new discussion post in which you:
    • Highlight behavioural indicators that provide good alignment with the corresponding Graduate Attributes' competencies.
    • Identify which of Belshaw's eight essential elements are represented in the five Graduate Attributes?
    • Discuss which indicators you would be best placed to teach in your programme.

This activity is part of the Digital Literacy Frameworks section of the Introduction Module.
